The Ultimate Skincare Routine for Melanin-Rich Skin

March 15, 2026 · 5 min read · Skincare Tips

If you have melanin-rich skin, you already know that generic skincare advice often misses the mark. Products recommended for "all skin types" frequently fail to address the specific concerns of Black and brown skin — hyperpigmentation, post-inflammatory dark spots, uneven tone, and the need for targeted moisture without heaviness. That's why I built this routine from scratch, specifically for my clients here at Rosie's Beauty Spa in Arlington, TX.

After six-plus years treating 800+ clients with melanin-rich skin, I've learned what actually works — and what makes things worse. Here's the complete morning and evening routine I recommend to every new Rosebud who walks through my door.

Morning Routine: Protect & Brighten

The morning routine has one primary job: protect. You're not treating dark spots in the morning — you're preventing new ones from forming.

Step 1: Gentle Hydrating Cleanser

Start with a gentle, hydrating cleanser that removes overnight buildup without stripping your moisture barrier. For melanin-rich skin, harsh cleansers are a common mistake. They compromise the barrier, which triggers excess melanin production — the exact opposite of what we want. Look for gel or cream cleansers with ceramides or hyaluronic acid. Avoid anything with sulfates as the first ingredient.

Step 2: Vitamin C Serum

Vitamin C is the #1 ingredient I recommend for hyperpigmentation and dark spots on melanin-rich skin. It inhibits the enzyme that triggers melanin overproduction, brightens existing discoloration, and protects against UV-induced pigmentation. Apply 2–3 drops to clean, dry skin and wait 60 seconds before moving to the next step. Look for L-ascorbic acid at 10–15% concentration, or a more stable derivative like ascorbyl glucoside if you have sensitive skin.

Step 3: SPF 30+ (Non-Negotiable)

I hear it all the time: "I don't need SPF, I have melanin." That's a myth I spend a lot of time correcting. Melanin does offer some natural protection — roughly SPF 2–4 — but it's nowhere near enough. UV radiation is the #1 trigger for dark spots and hyperpigmentation in all skin tones. Without SPF, every other step in this routine becomes significantly less effective. Choose a tinted mineral SPF or a chemical formula that doesn't leave a white cast.

Evening Routine: Treat & Repair

The evening routine is where the real corrective work happens. Your skin repairs itself while you sleep, making nighttime the optimal window for active ingredients.

Step 1: Double Cleanse

If you wear SPF (which you do now), double cleansing is essential. Start with an oil-based cleanser or micellar water to break down sunscreen and any buildup, then follow with your gentle hydrating cleanser. This ensures actives applied afterward can actually penetrate the skin — not sit on top of residue.

Step 2: Retinol or Treatment Serum

This is where you treat your specific concerns. Retinol accelerates cell turnover, fading dark spots and smoothing uneven texture — but it requires careful introduction on melanin-rich skin. Start at a low concentration (0.025%–0.05%) just twice a week, and build up slowly. Rushing this step can cause irritation, which triggers post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation — more dark spots. If retinol feels too aggressive, tranexamic acid or azelaic acid are excellent gentler alternatives for hyperpigmentation.

Note: This is exactly the step where Ashley's professional treatments accelerate your results. The corrective peels and serums used in-spa are significantly more potent than what's available over the counter — and are customized to your skin's specific needs.

Step 3: Rich Moisturizer

Melanin-rich skin often has a stronger moisture barrier than lighter skin tones, but active ingredients can disrupt that. Lock everything in with a nourishing moisturizer that contains ceramides, niacinamide, and peptides. Niacinamide does double duty — it's a potent brightening agent and a barrier-strengthening ingredient. Apply while skin is still slightly damp for maximum absorption.
